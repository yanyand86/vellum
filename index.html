Tips

Tidy handwriting, block by block. Switch to Type and tap anywhere on the page to drop a text block. Write in it with your pencil — iPadOS Scribble converts it to text, and Vellum sets it in that block's own font, size and colour. Every block is independent, so a heading can be Caveat and a note beside it Kalam.

Move things about. Tap a block to select it, then drag the round grip to reposition and the square grip to change its width. Blocks land neatly on the ruled lines.

Room to think. Pinch to zoom, and drag with one finger to pan once a pencil has been seen. Landscape pages suit mind-maps; the focus button hides everything but the paper.

Shape snapping. With Snap on, draw a shape and hold the nib still at the end — your wobbly circle, box, triangle, arrow or line becomes a clean one, still in your chosen ink.

Lasso. Pick the lasso, circle some ink, then move, resize, duplicate, recolour or delete it.

Ink. Fountain, brush and calligraphy nibs respond to pressure; pencil and pastel shade wider as you tilt. Scribble lives in Settings ▸ Apple Pencil. Squeeze, double-tap and barrel-roll stay with native apps — Safari doesn't receive them.

Text that runs off the page. A block that outgrows the paper is outlined in amber. Tap it and press the ⤓ button to pour the overflow onto the next page — it breaks on a word, keeps the font, and lands in the same column.

Sync. The cloud button on the shelf links Vellum to your own Firebase Realtime Database, so notebooks follow you between iPad and iPhone. Edits merge page by page and the newest wins, so two devices touching different pages never fight. Notes are written on-device first, so everything keeps working offline and syncs when you're back.
